The Creator

About Aluca Sol

The maker behind Triarchs of Olympus, and the intent behind the project.

Aluca Sol

Aluca Sol is the creator and sole developer of Triarchs of Olympus, built from the ground up as a browser-based, three-player free-for-all MOBA. The project's own record — its code, its README, and the match itself — is the clearest account of the intent behind it, so this page keeps close to that rather than reaching for biographical detail that isn't documented anywhere in the project.

Why Triarchs of Olympus Exists

Most MOBAs split their players into two fixed teams. Triarchs of Olympus asks a different question: what happens when three sides fight for the same mountain, with no alliance guaranteed to hold? The result is a symmetric triangular battlefield — authored once as a single sector and mirrored three ways — so that no empire ever fights on unfair ground.

The game was also built to be frictionless to actually play: matches connect players directly to one another with no account system and no backend server to maintain, filled out with AI opponents when human players are short. Every match resets completely when it ends; only local preferences, like AI difficulty or settings, are remembered between games.

Design Philosophy

The project keeps its entire balance model — hero stats, ability numbers, mob strength, gold and experience curves — in one central configuration, tuned as a single source of truth rather than scattered constants. Its jungle is hand-authored rather than randomly generated, with deliberate camps, chokepoints and grass thickets meant to reward map awareness. Every hero was designed around a distinct role and a five-ability kit that unlocks gradually across a match, so a champion's identity sharpens as the game goes on rather than arriving all at once.
